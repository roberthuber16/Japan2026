Phase 1 · Tokyo
Days 1–7 · Oct 31 – Nov 6 · Base: Aman Tokyo or The Peninsula
Day 1 · Oct 31 Arrival & Akihabara night Anime ▼
Afternoon
Check in & decompress
Settle into your hotel. Aman Tokyo's rooms overlook the Imperial Palace gardens — a stunning first view of the city.
Aman Tokyo · from ¥120,000/night
Evening
Akihabara Electric Town
Ease in gently with a walk through neon-lit Akihabara — arcades, anime goods, retro game shops. Dinner at a standing sushi bar nearby.
Day 2 · Nov 1 Shinjuku, Meiji & Harajuku Culture ▼
Morning
Meiji Jingu Shrine
Forested Shinto shrine in the heart of the city. Early morning is tranquil. The walk through the cedar forest sets a meditative tone.
Midday
Harajuku & Omotesando
Takeshita Street for wild street fashion and crepes. Then the upscale Omotesando boulevard — Tokyo's answer to the Champs-Élysées.
Evening
Shinjuku izakaya crawl
Golden Gai's tiny six-seat bars, each with its own personality. Book ahead at Nakamura for excellent yakitori in Omoide Yokocho (Memory Lane).
Bar-hop budget ¥5,000–¥10,000/person
Day 3 · Nov 2 TeamLab & Shibuya Art & Design ▼
Morning
TeamLab Planets, Toyosu
Immersive digital art installation — wade through water rooms, walk through mirrored infinity spaces. Book first-entry tickets in advance.
¥3,200/person · book ahead
Afternoon
Shibuya Crossing & Sky
The famous scramble crossing at street level, then up to Shibuya Sky observation deck for the bird's-eye view of controlled chaos.
Evening
Omakase dinner
Reserve at Saito or Sushi Sho for a classic high-end omakase. These seats book out months ahead — arrange before you travel.
¥40,000–¥80,000/person
Day 4 · Nov 3 Nikko day trip Temples ▼
Early AM
Shinkansen to Nikko
2 hrs north of Tokyo. Toshogu Shrine complex — elaborately gilded mausoleums set in cedar forest with autumn foliage at peak intensity in early November.
Afternoon
Kegon Falls & Lake Chuzenji
Stunning 97m waterfall with the autumn-coloured hills behind it. One of Japan's most photogenic scenes in November.
Evening
Return to Tokyo
Late dinner at a neighborhood ramen-ya near your hotel — a good reset after a big day.
Days 5–6 · Nov 4–5 Deep Tokyo: art, whisky & design Art & Design ▼
Day 5
Mori Art Museum & Roppongi
Contemporary art at altitude on the 53rd floor. Roppongi Hills has excellent galleries — pair with the National Art Center nearby.
Day 5 Eve
Whisky bar in Ginza
Bar High Five or The SG Club are world-class bartending institutions. Rare Japanese whisky (Karuizawa, Hanyu Ichiro's) poured by masters.
¥3,000–¥15,000/dram
Day 6
Yanaka & Shimokitazawa
Yanaka is old Tokyo — narrow lanes, temples, cat cafes, pottery shops. Shimokitazawa for vinyl record shops, vintage clothing, and live jazz.
Day 6 Eve
Tsukiji Outer Market dinner
The outer market (still operating) has some of Tokyo's best casual seafood. Tamagoyaki, fresh uni, grilled scallops.
Day 7 · Nov 6 Akihabara deep dive & prep Anime ▼
All day
Akihabara proper
Yodobashi Camera, Mandarake Complex, Super Potato retro games, Radio Kaikan for figures and doujinshi. A full day disappears here easily.
Evening
Pack for Alps leg
Tomorrow you leave for the Japan Alps. Send luggage ahead via takkyubin courier service — travel light on the train.
Phase 2 · Japan Alps & Kanazawa
Days 8–11 · Nov 7–10 · Hakone → Matsumoto → Kanazawa
Day 8 · Nov 7 Hakone — Mt. Fuji views Nature ▼
Morning
Romancecar train to Hakone
The iconic Odakyu Romancecar from Shinjuku — panoramic front windows, seats face the view. Mt. Fuji often visible on clear November mornings.
Afternoon
Hakone Open Air Museum
Sculptures set against mountain scenery — a Picasso pavilion, Rodin bronzes, a full Henry Moore. Foliage backdrop in November is stunning.
Evening
Kaiseki dinner & onsen
Check into Gora Kadan or Hoshino Resorts KAI Hakone for a multi-course kaiseki dinner and private outdoor onsen with mountain views.
Gora Kadan · from ¥80,000/night w/meals
Day 9 · Nov 8 Hakone Loop & Matsumoto Nature & History ▼
Morning
Hakone Loop — ropeway & lake
Cable car over volcanic Owakudani steam vents, then Lake Ashi cruise with Fuji reflection (weather permitting). Buy the black sulfur egg.
Afternoon
Train to Matsumoto
Transfer toward the Alps. Matsumoto Castle — "Crow Castle" — is Japan's finest surviving original castle, jet black against the Northern Alps.
Evening
Nawate street & sake bar
Frog-themed Nawate-dori is a charming old shopping street. Excellent local Nagano sake in the evening at a small neighborhood bar.
Day 10 · Nov 9 Shirakawa-go UNESCO Village Temples & Culture ▼
All day
Shirakawa-go gassho farmhouses
UNESCO-listed mountain village with steep-thatched farmhouses. In early November the surrounding hills are peak autumn colour. Visit Wada House inside.
Evening
Arrive Kanazawa
Check into Hotel Kanazawa or Hyatt Centric Kanazawa. Known as "little Kyoto" — geisha districts, samurai neighborhoods, incredible food scene.
Day 11 · Nov 10 Kanazawa — Kenroku-en & Higashi Chaya Culture ▼
Morning
Kenroku-en Garden
Considered one of Japan's three great gardens. In November the iconic yukitsuri (rope-supported) pine trees are installed for winter — a beautiful and unique sight.
Afternoon
Higashi Chaya geisha district
The best-preserved geisha district outside Kyoto. Walk the stone-paved lanes, tour a restored ochaya teahouse. Superb gold leaf craft shops.
Evening
Omicho Market & seafood dinner
Kanazawa seafood is world-class — red snow crab, nodoguro (blackthroat sea perch) and fatty yellow sea urchin are the must-orders.
Splurge on nodoguro — worth every yen
Phase 3 · Kyoto
Days 12–16 · Nov 11–15 · Base: Tawaraya Ryokan or Suiran
Day 12 · Nov 11 Arrive Kyoto — Arashiyama Nature ▼
Morning
Shinkansen Kanazawa → Kyoto
Under an hour on the Thunderbird / Shinkansen transfer. Arrive, check in at Tawaraya — the most celebrated ryokan in Japan, guests include celebrities and heads of state.
Tawaraya · from ¥120,000/night w/meals
Afternoon
Arashiyama bamboo grove
The famous bamboo path is quieter in the afternoon. Tenryu-ji garden next door has spectacular maple foliage in November. Walk the Togetsukyo bridge over the Oi River.
Evening
Kaiseki at the ryokan
Your first multi-course kaiseki dinner served in your room. Seasonal November ingredients: matsutake mushrooms, Kyoto vegetables (kyo-yasai), fresh river fish.
Day 13 · Nov 12 Fushimi Inari & Gion at dusk Temples ▼
Dawn
Fushimi Inari — sunrise hike
Arrive at 6am. The endless vermillion torii gates with autumn leaves and morning mist, nearly deserted — one of Japan's most magical experiences.
Afternoon
Philosopher's Path
The canal-side stone path lined with maples in full autumn colour. Links Nanzen-ji, Eikan-do, and Ginkaku-ji — each exceptional in November.
Dusk
Gion at twilight
Walk the Hanamikoji and Shirakawa canal streets in fading light. If lucky, spot a maiko (apprentice geisha) heading to an evening engagement.
Day 14 · Nov 13 Nara day trip Nature & Temples ▼
Morning
Todai-ji Great Buddha
45 min from Kyoto. The Great Buddha Hall is the world's largest wooden structure. Free-roaming deer bow for crackers throughout the park.
Afternoon
Kasuga Taisha & Isuien Garden
Kasuga's lantern-lined approach is enchanting. Isuien is a lesser-known gem — a strolling garden framing Todai-ji's roof in its composition.
Evening
Return & sake tasting
Back in Kyoto — sake tasting at Fushimi, Japan's second-largest sake brewing district, just south of the city.
Days 15–16 · Nov 14–15 Kinkaku-ji, Kodo & tea ceremony Culture ▼
Day 15 AM
Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)
Go early before tour buses arrive. The gold-leaf pavilion reflected in the mirror pond with autumn maples is genuinely breathtaking.
Day 15 PM
Private tea ceremony
Book a private ura-senke school tea ceremony. Far more intimate and authentic than tourist versions — involves a full multi-course wagashi sweet pairing.
~¥20,000/person private experience
Day 16
Kodo incense ceremony & Nishiki Market
A Kodo (way of incense) session is one of Japan's three classical arts, rarely experienced by tourists. Then Nishiki Market — "Kyoto's Kitchen" — for pickles, tofu, and street snacks.
Phase 4 · Osaka, Hiroshima & Naoshima
Days 17–19 · Nov 16–18
Day 17 · Nov 16 Osaka — eat everything Food ▼
Morning
Dotonbori & Kuromon Market
Osaka's food culture is its identity. Kuromon Ichiba market for fresh crab and pufferfish. Dotonbori for takoyaki, kushikatsu, and the glowing Glico man.
Evening
Hozenji Yokocho & sake bar
Narrow moss-walled alley with atmospheric restaurants. Fugu (pufferfish) dinner at a licensed restaurant — a bucket-list Japanese meal.
Fugu course ¥15,000–¥30,000/person
Day 18 · Nov 17 Hiroshima & Miyajima History ▼
Morning
Peace Memorial Park & Museum
Allow 2–3 hours. The museum is one of the most affecting in the world. The A-Bomb Dome ruin alongside the Motoyasu River remains deeply moving.
Afternoon
Miyajima Island & floating torii
A 10-minute ferry. Itsukushima Shrine's torii gate appears to float at high tide. November foliage on Mt. Misen is superb. Take the ropeway up.
Evening
Okonomiyaki dinner
Hiroshima-style okonomiyaki (layered, not mixed) is completely different from Osaka style — a signature local dish not to miss.
Day 19 · Nov 18 Naoshima Art Island Art & Design ▼
All day
Benesse Art Site
Naoshima is one of the world's great art experiences. Chichu Art Museum (Monet Water Lilies in a purpose-built underground space), Lee Ufan Museum, and Art House Project in the old village.
Stay at Benesse House overnight if available — ¥80,000+
Phase 5 · Tokyo Return
Days 20–22 · Nov 19–21 · Final stretch + departure
Days 20–21 · Nov 19–20 Tokyo finale — last meals & shopping Food ▼
Day 20
Toyosu Market tuna auction
Apply in advance for the early morning tuna auction lottery (5am). Even without winning, watching from the gallery is spectacular. Sushi breakfast immediately after is mandatory.
Daiwa Sushi in the market building — arrive by 5:30am
Day 20 PM
Ginza shopping
Itoya (10-floor stationery paradise), Uniqlo flagship, luxury fashion. Dover Street Market Tokyo for rare streetwear and art objects.
Day 21
Odaiba & teamLab Borderless
TeamLab Borderless reopened in a new Azabudai Hills location — different from Planets, more expansive wandering experience. Evening: final splurge dinner at Joël Robuchon Tokyo.
Robuchon Tokyo · ¥50,000+/person
Day 22 · Nov 21 Departure Luxury ▼
Morning
Narita or Haneda transfer
Take the Narita Express or Airport Limousine Bus. Allow 2.5 hours before departure. The JAL / ANA first class lounges at Haneda are exceptional — worth arriving early.
Haneda closer to central Tokyo — check your departure terminal
